# 01 — Where You Are (Honest Snapshot)

Not a problem statement. Not a "look at the damage." Just the truth — strengths first, gaps named, no exaggeration either way.

## What you have to build with

**A real, marketable skill.**
Web and app development. This earns you 60,000–90,000 BDT a month freelancing from home. That's not nothing — it's the foundation everything else is built on. In 5 years, this skill alone is worth ~50 lakh BDT of earning capacity. Treat it like an asset that pays dividends.

**Working freelance income.**
You're not unemployed. You're not stuck. You're producing. Whether the world calls it modest or great, monthly income arriving every month is the rarest thing on earth — most people don't have it.

**Time + willingness to learn.**
You're reading this. That alone puts you in the top 5% of people in any financial situation. Most people in your situation refuse to look at money at all. You're choosing to look.

**A family.**
A spouse and a kid. They're a reason to do this and a support system. With family aligned, learning + practicing becomes a shared project, not a lonely grind.

**Health and time.**
You're not sick. You have hours each day. You have years ahead. These are the actually-valuable assets — money is downstream of them.

## What you don't yet have — and that's normal

**Finance knowledge.** Until you've sat down and studied what compound interest, inflation, and real return actually mean in your life, you can't make good decisions about them. This isn't a flaw — it's a gap that learning closes. Stage 0 of the roadmap.

**Tracking habits.** Right now, you probably don't know exactly where your money goes each month. Most people don't. Stage 1 fixes this — not by guessing, but by tracking 30 days and seeing.

**Investing experience.** No DPS, no FDR, no stocks. That's OK — nobody starts with these. Stage 3 is where small experiments begin. Not now.

**Savings.** Zero today. This is uncomfortable but not unusual. Savings is an *output* of knowledge + discipline + income. Build the inputs first, output follows.

## What is also true (just noted, not the focus)

You carry approximately 7,00,000 BDT in debt across four buckets: credit card, personal loan, family/informal, and a market/chit-fund loan. The chit-fund one in particular is at a high effective rate (often 5%/month in Bangladesh = 60%+ APR) and is the kind of debt that deserves your awareness if not your panic.

**This is one chapter, not the whole book.** You'll learn how debt actually works in Stage 0, build awareness of yours in Stage 1, and clear it from growing surplus as Stages 2 and 3 do their work. We don't restructure your life around it. We let knowledge and earning do the work — and the math eventually catches up to the discipline.

See `10-debt-awareness.md` when you want to think about debt specifically.

## The math, gently

A working freelancer earning 70k/month, learning continuously, slowly raising rates, and starting to invest small amounts — over 5 years that person typically:
- Doubles their income (skill + niche + productizing)
- Builds 3–6 months emergency fund
- Has a small but real investment portfolio
- Has cleared most or all of their high-interest debt as a side-effect

You don't need to be smart, rich, or lucky. You need to be consistent and curious. Both are choices.
