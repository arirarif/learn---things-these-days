## What investing actually is (and isn't)

**Investing is:** putting money into something that produces *more* money over time — a business, a piece of property, a fractional ownership in many businesses (stocks), a loan to a government (bonds), or a productive asset.

**Investing is NOT:**
- Trading (buying low and selling high quickly) — that's speculation
- Forex / crypto — those aren't investments, they're gambling on currency moves
- "Forex signals" / "stock tips" / Telegram groups — those are scams
- Putting money under your mattress — that loses to inflation

**The difference between investing and speculating:**
- Investing: you own a piece of something *productive*. Whether or not the price moves daily, the thing is *making* money (a company earns profits, a building collects rent, etc.)
- Speculating: you're hoping the price moves in your favor. There's no productive activity underneath. It's a bet.

When `doc.md` and this folder talk about "investing", they always mean the *real* kind — owning productive things.

## When you'll be ready (the honest answer)

You're ready when **all** of these are true:

1. **You've completed Stage 0** — the 10 concepts feel real, not academic
2. **You've completed Stage 1** — you have 3+ months of tracking data showing your actual surplus
3. **You have a small emergency fund** — at least one month of expenses in a savings account that you don't touch
4. **You've stopped using your credit card to live on** — i.e., your card isn't growing the debt
5. **You're earning a stable monthly income** that hasn't crashed in 6 months

Most people who skip these are *gambling*, not investing — even if they call it investing. The above 5 items are the foundation that turns a money-loser into a money-grower.

For you, realistically, this is **6–18 months from today**, depending on how Stages 0–2 go.

## What you absolutely will NOT do (the avoid list)

### Things that look like investing but aren't

- ❌ **Forex trading** — illegal in Bangladesh under FERA. Even where legal, 90%+ of retail traders lose long-term.
- ❌ **Crypto trading** — same legal status, same loss rate.
- ❌ **Day trading any market** — same statistics, more taxes.
- ❌ **"Sure-shot stock tip" Telegram / Facebook groups** — pump-and-dump schemes. The person running the group sells while you buy.
- ❌ **Binary options / FX signals services** — outright scams.

### "Get rich" sales pitches

- ❌ **MLM / network marketing** (Tiens, Vestige, GoldQuest, etc.) — math doesn't work. Only the top 0.1% make money, and they make it from recruiting the bottom 99.9%.
- ❌ **"Stock market mastery" courses** for 10–50k BDT — most are recycled YouTube content at 100x markup.
- ❌ **"Forex master class" / "crypto millionaire blueprint"** — same.
- ❌ **Real estate "investment" seminars** with hard sells — the seminar is the product.

### Debt traps disguised as opportunities

- ❌ **Margin trading / borrowing to invest** — turns normal losses into catastrophic losses
- ❌ **More credit cards "to invest in a deal"** — the deal is the trap
- ❌ **Borrowing from one informal lender to "invest" in something** — 99% of the time you lose both the money and the relationship

### The one rule that beats all of the above

> **If someone shows you returns of more than 15% per year, "guaranteed" or "almost guaranteed", they are lying. Walk away.**

Real long-term equity returns are 8–15% per year *on average* with significant year-to-year swings. Anything above that range with the word "guaranteed" is either luck (won't repeat), leverage (won't last), or fraud (won't end well).

## The mindset that makes investing work

A few principles to internalize before you ever buy anything:

### 1. Time in the market beats timing the market
You will never reliably predict whether the market goes up or down next week. Long-term, markets go up. The only reliable way to capture that is to *be invested* for a long time, regardless of short-term moves.

### 2. Boring is good
The most successful investors don't do anything exciting. They put money in steadily, hold for decades, and let compound interest do the work. If your investing strategy is exciting, you're probably gambling.

### 3. You can't "make up for lost time"
The single biggest factor in wealth is *how long* your money compounds, not how cleverly it's invested. Starting at 30 with 1,000 BDT/month beats starting at 40 with 5,000 BDT/month — almost every time. The lesson: start small, start now (once you're ready). Don't wait to start "big."

### 4. Diversification > brilliance
You don't need to pick the next Apple. You need to own a basket of decent businesses for a long time. One stock can go to zero. A basket of 30 large companies almost never does.

### 5. Match risk to your life stage
You have a family and debt. That means you can't take large losses. Stick to low-to-medium risk instruments (DPS, Sanchaypatra, large-cap halal stocks) for the foreseeable future. The exciting high-return stuff is for people with no obligations and surplus to lose.
