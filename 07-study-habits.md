# 07 — Study Habits (Daily / Weekly / Monthly)

This is a **learning roadmap**, not a debt-payment one. So the habits revolve around *learning*, *practicing*, and *reviewing*. Money flows from these — not the other way around.

Total time budget: ~5 hours/week. Less in Stage 0 (more reading, less doing). More in Stage 2 (less reading, more building income).

---

## Daily (15 minutes, before bed)

**Two things:**

1. **Log today's expenses** (5 min) — in the tracker's Expenses tab, or your phone app. Every taka. No exception. This is the only non-negotiable.
2. **Study 10 minutes** — a finance video, a chapter, a concept, an annual report (later stages). Even 10 minutes builds the habit. Tap the Concepts tab to update what you've learned.

That's it. 15 minutes daily. Below 15 = compounding habit. Above 15 = bonus.

**Why daily:** what you do every day shapes you. What you do once in a while is entertainment.

---

## Weekly (Sunday morning, 30 minutes, with a cup of tea)

1. **Review the week in the tracker** (10 min)
   - Look at Expenses tab — categorize what's untagged, look at totals
   - Look at Income tab — what came in, from where
   - Look at Concepts tab — what did you learn this week?
2. **Plan the next 7 days of study** (5 min) — which concept, which video, which chapter
3. **Money chat with spouse** (10 min) — what surprised you this week, what's the plan for next, anything she noticed
4. **Note one thing** in the Notes tab — a lesson, a question, a worry, a win. Tag it with `#lesson` `#win` `#question` etc.

**Why weekly:** weekly cadence catches drift before it becomes damage. And turns a solo grind into a shared project.

---

## Monthly (1st of month, 1 hour)

1. **Net Worth snapshot** (15 min) — go to Net Worth tab, log balances. Watch the number move over time. Even when negative.
2. **Debt snapshot** (10 min) — Debt tab → log total debt at month-end. You're not attacking it yet (Stage 0–1) but you're aware.
3. **Income vs expense for the month** (10 min) — was there surplus? Deficit? Why?
4. **Read 1 chapter / watch 1 long video** (20 min) — pick from the resources list for your stage
5. **Set learning intention for next month** (5 min) — write it in the Notes tab. Examples: *"Master inflation deeply"*, *"Read chapter 8 of Psychology of Money"*, *"Watch 3 BO account videos"*

**Why monthly:** Month is the natural cycle for income, bills, and rent. Reviewing monthly aligns with how money actually flows.

---

## Quarterly (every 3 months, 2 hours)

1. **Income review** — has freelance rate increased? Is it time to quote new clients higher?
2. **Skill review** — what's next to learn on the craft side? (Stage 2 cares about this a lot)
3. **Curriculum audit** — are you applying what you've read, or just consuming?
4. **One experiment** — try one new thing: a niche post, a productized service quote, a small investment, anything

**Why quarterly:** Long enough to see real trends, short enough to course-correct.

---

## Annually (1 day, birthday or new year)

1. **Full review** with spouse — where were we 12 months ago, where now?
2. **Stage check** — which roadmap stage are you actually in? Be honest.
3. **3 wins, 3 lessons, 3 plans** — write them in the Notes tab
4. **Update the plan** if needed (this folder is yours — edit it)
5. **Celebrate** — even small progress deserves it. Dinner, a small gift, anything.

---

## Non-negotiable habits (the minimum)

If everything else falls apart, hold these three:

1. **Daily expense log** — 5 minutes
2. **Sunday review** — 10 minutes with spouse
3. **Monthly net worth snapshot** — 30 minutes

That's 75 minutes of total non-negotiable discipline per month. Anyone who says they don't have time for that is choosing not to.

---

## Automation — let machines do what they can

- Standing bank instruction so EMIs auto-debit (you don't have to remember)
- Auto-transfer 10% to savings on payday (when Stage 3 starts)
- Weekly Sunday calendar reminder for the money chat
- Bookmark `tracker.html` on phone home screen — one tap to log

Automation is how you make good behavior easier than bad behavior.

---

## When you fall off (and you will)

Don't restart at 100%. Restart at 1.

If you missed a week of tracking, log today. Don't try to reconstruct the past. Don't beat yourself up. Don't quit.

The people who win aren't perfect — they just come back faster.

---

## One sentence that holds it all

> **"15 minutes a day for 5 years beats 5 hours a day for 1 month — every single time. The plan only works if it survives the bad weeks."**
