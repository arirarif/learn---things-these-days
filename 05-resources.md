# 05 — Learning Resources

Books, videos, channels, apps — in the right order for the right stage. Use these **only when the stage calls for them**. Don't binge in Stage 0 — track expenses and learn the 10 concepts instead.

---

## Stage 0–1 — Foundation behavior + first concepts

### Bengali YouTube (start here)
- **Adib's Finance** — budgeting, debt, basic finance for Bangladeshis. Clear, calm, no hype.
- **10 Minute School** (Ayman Sadiq) — skill development + finance modules. Polished, accessible.
- **Finance Bangla** — investing basics in Bengali
- **Khan Academy Bangla** — economics and finance fundamentals (best for math-heavy concepts)

### English YouTube (Indian, very relatable)
- **Labour Law Advisor** (LLA) — budgeting, EPF, taxes, no-nonsense
- **CA Rachana Phadke Ranade** — investing basics, very clear
- **Pranjal Kamra** — mutual funds, investing mindset
- **Asset Yogi** — real estate + general finance

### Apps for expense tracking
- **Wallet** (Budgetbakers) — clean interface, free tier sufficient
- **Money Lover** — popular, free tier sufficient
- **Google Sheets** — most reliable, no ads (use the templates in this folder)
- Your own **`tracker.html`** — the dashboard built into this project

## Stage 2 — First serious books (when concepts are solid)

### Bengali / translated to Bengali
- **টাকার মনস্তত্ত্ব** (*The Psychology of Money* by Morgan Housel, Bengali translation) — on Rokomari. **The single best first finance book.** Mindset before mechanics.
- *Rich Dad Poor Dad* (Bengali version available) — some advice is dated; read for mindset shift only, not specific tactics

### English
- **The Psychology of Money** — Morgan Housel (read first if you can read English well; the original is short and easy)
- **I Will Teach You to Be Rich** — Ramit Sethi (practical, behavior-focused)
- **Your Money or Your Life** — Vicki Robin (deep on the time-money relationship)

### How to actually read these
- One chapter per week (not per day — let it land)
- Write a paragraph in the Notes tab after each chapter
- Discuss with spouse on Sunday review

## Stage 3 — Investing fundamentals

### Bangladesh-specific (you'll actually use these)
- **DSE official site** (dsebd.org) — daily prices, company financials, annual reports
- **LankaBangla** / **City Brokerage** / **Royal Capital** / **EBL Securities** — broker apps for BO accounts
- **Bangladesh Bank Sanchaypatra info** — official sanchaypatra.gov.bd
- **IBBL, Al-Arafah, EXIM, City Islami, First Security Islami** — Shariah-compliant DPS / Mudaraba options. Visit their websites; some now have online account opening.

### Books — investing
- **The Little Book of Common Sense Investing** — John Bogle (index investing logic; short, life-changing)
- **One Up on Wall Street** — Peter Lynch (how to think about stocks as a regular person)
- **The Bogleheads' Guide to Investing** — practical, friendly

### Halal finance specifically
- **An Introduction to Islamic Finance** — Mufti Taqi Usmani (the standard reference)
- **Wahed Invest** website + blog — the world's first global halal robo-advisor. Even if you can't use it from Bangladesh easily, the educational content is great.
- **AAOIFI** standards (only if you're going deep professionally)

## Time allocation by stage

| Stage | Time/week on learning content |
|---|---|
| 0 — Foundation | 2–3 hours — concept-by-concept, slow |
| 1 — Practice | 1–2 hours — most time goes to tracking |
| 2 — Income | 5 hours — heavily product/sales/niche |
| 3 — Foundation investing | 3 hours — books + DSE observation |
| 4 — Deep wealth | 3–5 hours — books and analysis |
| 5 — Advanced | as much as you want — you're a learner now |

**Important:** these are time budgets, not hard limits. The key is *consistency*, not volume. 30 minutes every day beats 5 hours one Sunday and nothing else.

## The four-question filter for any new resource

Before consuming any video, book, course, or article, ask:

1. **What stage am I in, and is this resource for that stage?**
2. **Will I be able to apply this in the next 30 days?** If not, save it for later.
3. **Is this teaching, or is it selling something?** Selling courses, services, or "memberships" is a red flag for trustworthiness.
4. **Will this still be true in 5 years?** If it's chasing a trend (NFTs, AI-stock-bots, etc.), skip.

If a resource passes all four, it earns your time.
