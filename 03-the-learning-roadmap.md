# 03 — The 5-Stage Learning Roadmap

This is the spine of everything. Read it once now. Re-read it at the start of each month. **The order matters more than the speed.**

The pace below is *typical* — go faster if you have time, slower if life gets busy. The point is to never skip a stage, not to hit the timeline.

---

## STAGE 0 — Learn the Language (Months 1–2)

**Goal:** internalize 10 foundational finance concepts. No money moves. No big decisions. Just *understand*.

You can't make good decisions about cash flow, debt, savings, or investing if these words don't *mean* something concrete to you. Most people skip this stage and spend their whole lives confused. You won't.

### The 10 concepts to actually learn (in this order)
1. **Cash flow** — money in vs money out, monthly
2. **Net worth** — what you have minus what you owe
3. **Compound interest** — interest earning interest (your enemy on debt, your friend on savings)
4. **Inflation** — money losing value over time
5. **Real return** = nominal return − inflation
6. **Time value of money** — 1 taka today ≠ 1 taka in 5 years
7. **Asset vs liability** — what puts money in your pocket vs takes it out
8. **Income vs wealth** — income is what comes in monthly, wealth is what compounds over time
9. **Risk vs return** — higher return = higher risk; "guaranteed" high return is a lie
10. **Halal vs haram finance** — Shariah-compliant vs interest-based (you'll need this in Stage 3+)

Each concept gets one entry in `09-concepts.md` (the glossary). Read 1–2 per week, watch a video on it, write your own one-paragraph explanation in your own words. *If you can't explain it simply, you haven't learned it yet.*

### Habits to start in Stage 0
- Daily: read or watch 15 minutes of finance content
- Weekly: write one paragraph in your own words about what you learned
- Track the 10 concepts in the tracker's **Concepts** tab — tick off as you understand each

### Stage 0 success criteria
- You can explain each of the 10 concepts to your spouse in plain Bengali
- You've finished reading either *The Psychology of Money* (Bengali — টাকার মনস্তত্ত্ব) or watched ~10 hours of Adib's Finance / 10 Minute School finance content
- You're not afraid of words like "compound", "principal", "APR", "ROI", "inflation"

**Books / channels for Stage 0** — see `05-resources.md`. Stage 0 picks: টাকার মনস্তত্ত্ব, Adib's Finance, 10 Minute School.

## STAGE 1 — Practice Tracking (Month 2–3)

**Goal:** see your actual numbers. Reality replaces guesswork.

Now you have the concepts. Apply one of them — cash flow — to your own life. Track every taka in and out for 30 consecutive days. Most people who do this for the first time are *shocked* by what they find. That shock is the lesson.

### What to track
- **Every expense.** Use the **Expenses tab** of `tracker.html`. Or Wallet/Money Lover app on phone. Whatever you'll actually use.
  - Categorize: Rent, Kid, Food (groceries vs eating out), Utilities, Transport, Personal, Subscriptions, EMI payments, Other
  - Tag each as "Need" or "Want"
- **Every income.** Use the **Income tab**. Each client payment, with platform fees noted.
- **Your debts.** Use the **Debt tab** — fill exact amounts and rates. This isn't to attack them yet, just to know them. See `10-debt-awareness.md`.

### Habits to add in Stage 1
- Daily: 5-minute expense log before bed
- Weekly: Sunday 30-min review — categorize the week, plan the next
- Continue Stage 0 study habits

### Stage 1 success criteria
- 30 consecutive days of expense tracking complete
- You know your monthly average spend by category
- You know your needs % vs wants %
- You know exactly what each of your 4 debts looks like
- You've logged 1 net worth snapshot (it'll be negative — that's fine, you have a baseline)

You're not making big changes yet. You're just *seeing*. The seeing changes things on its own.

## STAGE 2 — Build the Earning Side (Month 3–9)

**Goal:** raise income through skill leverage, not by working more hours.

This is the longest stage and arguably the most important one. **Your skill is your single biggest financial asset.** Everything downstream — savings, investing, debt clearing — gets easier when income grows.

### The four rungs of earning leverage (climb them in order)

1. **Generalist hourly** ($5–15/hr) — where you probably are now
2. **Niche specialist** ($25–60/hr) — same hours, 2–3× rate, easier client acquisition
3. **Productized service** (fixed $500–5000 packages) — escapes hourly model
4. **Micro-SaaS** ($5–30/mo × N users) — software that earns while you sleep

See `08-skill-and-income.md` for the full deep-dive. Highlights:

### What to do this stage
- **Pick ONE niche** (not three). Score candidates on pain intensity, budget, reachability, competition, your edge. Total > 18/25 = pursue.
- **Raise rates 15–20% every quarter** for new clients. Existing clients with 30 days notice.
- **Productize one offer** by month 9 — turn an hourly service into a fixed-price package.
- **Continue learning** — but now reading finance only takes 1–2 hours/week. Most of your study time goes to your craft and your niche.

### Habits to add
- Weekly: 30 minutes "raising something" — niche post, portfolio piece, prospect outreach
- Monthly: review rate vs target progression
- Quarterly: raise rates by ~20% for new clients

### Stage 2 success criteria
- One niche chosen and posted in publicly (LinkedIn, Twitter, etc.) weekly
- Hourly rate at least 50% higher than start-of-Stage-2
- One productized offer live, even if not yet sold
- Monthly income trending up over a 6-month window

**Income mix target by end of Stage 2:** 80k baseline + 30–50k from niche/productized work = ~1.2–1.5 lakh BDT/month.

## STAGE 3 — Small Investing Experiments (Month 6–18)

**Goal:** learn investing the only way it actually sticks — by doing it with real money, in small amounts.

You're not Warren Buffett. You're a beginner. The point of Stage 3 is *experience*, not returns. The amounts are tiny on purpose so the lessons are cheap.

### What you actually do this stage

**Small DPS / Mudaraba Savings.** 500–2,000 BDT/month into a Shariah-compliant DPS (IBBL, Al-Arafah, EXIM, City Islami). 5–10 year tenure. **The point isn't the return** — it's getting comfortable with the idea of "money put away, not touched."

**Sanchaypatra.** When you have a few thousand spare, buy a 5-Year Bangladesh Sanchaypatra (~11% return, government-backed, near-zero risk). Spouse / child's name to maximize the limit.

**Open a BO account.** Through a broker (LankaBangla, City Brokerage, Royal Capital, etc.) Don't buy stocks for the first 6 months. Just watch.
- Watch Square Pharma, BATBC, Renata, Olympic Industries, GP — large-cap, mostly-halal Bangladeshi companies
- Read their annual reports — even just the first page. Get used to seeing balance sheets, income statements
- This is *learning by exposure*, not by buying

**After 6 months of watching:** buy 1,000–2,000 BDT of a single halal large-cap stock. That's all. Hold it. Watch it move. Read about why it moves.

### Habits to add
- Monthly: 30-minute "investing study" — read one Buffett shareholder letter, one annual report, or one chapter of *The Little Book of Common Sense Investing*
- Quarterly: log net worth snapshot, see investment portion grow

### Stage 3 success criteria
- DPS running 6+ months without missing a payment
- At least one Sanchaypatra purchased
- BO account open, observed DSE for 6+ months
- First halal stock purchased, held for 3+ months
- Comfortable explaining "diversification", "P/E ratio", and "dividend" in your own words

## STAGE 4 — Deeper Wealth Knowledge (Year 2+)

**Goal:** become the person who actually understands what they own and why.

By now you have: real tracking data, real income growth, real (if small) investing experience, real reduced debt as surplus has done its slow work. **You're not a beginner anymore.** Stage 4 is where the deeper books finally land.

### What to learn
- **Financial statement literacy** — read a balance sheet and income statement properly
- **Valuation basics** — what something is "worth" vs what it costs
- **Asset allocation** — diversification across stocks, sukuk, real estate, cash
- **Halal investing deeper** — Shariah screens, Islamic equity indexes (Dow Jones Islamic Market, FTSE Shariah)
- **Tax efficiency** — what you can legally do in Bangladesh to minimize tax drag

### Books that finally make sense at this stage
- *The Intelligent Investor* — Graham (chapters 8 and 20 are essential)
- *A Random Walk Down Wall Street* — Malkiel
- *The Most Important Thing* — Howard Marks
- *Common Stocks and Uncommon Profits* — Phil Fisher

### Habits
- Monthly: read one chapter of a serious investing book
- Quarterly: re-evaluate asset allocation vs goals
- Yearly: full financial review with spouse

---

## STAGE 5 — Compounding + Natural Debt Clearance (Year 3+)

**Goal:** wealth compounds. Debt finishes clearing as a natural consequence.

By Year 3, if you've followed the path:
- Your income is 2–3× starting (Stage 2 worked)
- You have real investments (Stage 3 compounded)
- You have deep knowledge (Stage 4 deposited)
- You have years of disciplined tracking habits

At this point, debt isn't a fight anymore. The chit-fund / market loan got addressed somewhere in Stages 2–3 (because at 60% APR even your gentle "slow accelerate" approach catches up to it once surplus is real). Credit card was eliminated as a habit, not a war. Personal loan ran out its schedule.

What you focus on in Stage 5:
- Systematic monthly investing (10–15% of income, automatic)
- International halal options if foreign currency allows
- Maybe real estate analysis (rental cash flow, not flipping)
- Possibly Islamic finance credentials (CSAA, Shariah audit) if you want the advisory career
- Building wealth that compounds for decades

---

## The order rule (memorize)

> **Learn → Practice tracking → Build earning skill → Small investing → Deeper knowledge → Compounding wealth (debt clears along the way)**
>
> Skip a stage and you'll come back to it. Trust the order.

---

## One sentence that fits all stages

> **"The goal isn't to fix your money. The goal is to become a person who naturally handles money well — and then watch what that person's life looks like in 5 years."**
