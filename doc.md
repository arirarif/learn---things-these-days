## The truth in what they said

Financial skills genuinely do build wealth more reliably than tech skills alone. Here's why:

- **Tech skills earn you income.** Financial skills turn income into wealth.
- Most high-earning developers stay middle-class their whole life because they never learn money. Most wealthy people aren't the highest earners — they're the best capital allocators.
- Financial literacy compounds across your entire life, not just your career years.
- AI can replace coding. AI cannot replace judgment about capital, risk, and ownership.

So yes — learning finance is one of the highest-ROI things any professional can do. **But** "financial skills" is a vague phrase that covers very different things, some of which are wealth-building and some of which are wealth-destroying.

## The actual financial skills that build wealth

**Tier 1 — Foundation (everyone needs these):**
- Personal finance: budgeting, saving discipline, avoiding debt traps
- Understanding cash flow vs net worth
- Tax optimization (legal)
- Basics of how money, inflation, and currency work

**Tier 2 — Wealth-building (this is where the leverage is):**
- **Investing fundamentals** — how to evaluate businesses, understand risk vs return
- **Reading financial statements** — balance sheets, income statements, cash flow
- **Valuation** — knowing what something is actually worth
- **Asset allocation** — diversification across asset classes
- **Real estate analysis** — cash flow, cap rates, financing
- **Business ownership / equity** — building or buying income-producing assets

**Tier 3 — Advanced (compounds massively over decades):**
- M&A, private equity thinking
- Tax structuring (legal entities, trusts, jurisdictions)
- Capital raising
- Building businesses that generate equity, not just income

## What people *think* is wealth-building but usually isn't

⚠️ **Day trading, forex, crypto trading** — over 90% lose money long-term. Don't confuse trading with investing.

⚠️ **"Get rich quick" courses on Instagram/YouTube** — most of these gurus make money selling courses, not from the skills they teach.

⚠️ **Stock tips, signals groups** — almost always a losing game.

⚠️ **Copying what "rich influencers" do** — survivorship bias. You only see the winners; the 95% who failed don't post.
