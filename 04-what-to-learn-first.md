# 04 — What to Learn First (and What to Skip)

The internet is full of finance advice. 99% of it isn't useful to you right now. This file tells you what *is* — and equally important, what you can safely ignore.

---

## Learn first (in this order)

### Tier A — The foundation (Stage 0, Months 1–2)
Master these or nothing else makes sense. See full entries in `09-concepts.md`.

1. **Cash flow** — money in vs out, monthly
2. **Net worth** — what you have minus what you owe
3. **Compound interest** — interest on interest, both directions
4. **Inflation** — money losing value over time
5. **Real return** = nominal return − inflation
6. **Time value of money** — today's taka ≠ next year's taka
7. **Asset vs liability** — Kiyosaki's definition
8. **Income vs wealth** — monthly inflow vs accumulated stock
9. **Risk vs return** — higher return = higher risk, no exceptions
10. **Halal vs haram finance** — Mudaraba, Sukuk, Riba

**How much time:** 30–60 min per concept across read + watch + write + teach. Total: 20–25 hours over 8 weeks. That's it.

### Tier B — Practical literacy (Stage 1, Month 2–3)
- How to read a personal budget
- How to track expenses by category
- The difference between fixed and variable expenses
- How to know your needs vs your wants

### Tier C — Earning skill (Stage 2, Month 3–9)
- How freelance rate-setting actually works
- Niche selection
- Productizing a service
- Building in public (LinkedIn, Twitter)
- Cold outreach basics

See `08-skill-and-income.md` for the deep version.

### Tier D — Investing (Stage 3, Month 6–18)
- What stocks, bonds, sukuk actually are
- How to read a basic balance sheet and income statement
- What diversification means in practice
- Dollar-cost averaging (or its Bangladesh equivalent)
- How DPS / Sanchaypatra work
- How DSE works at a basic level
- Halal screening (which stocks are Shariah-compliant, why)

See `11-investing-when-ready.md`.

### Tier E — Wealth knowledge (Stage 4, Year 2+)
- Valuation (price vs worth)
- Asset allocation
- Tax efficiency
- Estate / inheritance basics
- Deeper Islamic finance: AAOIFI standards, sukuk structures

### Tier F — Advanced (Stage 5, Year 3+)
- Real estate analysis
- Building income-producing businesses
- Capital structuring
- Optional: Islamic finance certifications (CSAA, Shariah audit) if you want the advisory career

## Don't bother with these (it's not for you, yet — or ever)

### Skip entirely
- ❌ **Day trading / forex / crypto trading** — illegal in Bangladesh + 90% lose long-term + it's not investing, it's gambling
- ❌ **Stock tip / signal Telegram groups** — pump-and-dump schemes
- ❌ **"Make $5,000/month with this one trick" YouTube** — clickbait, no substance
- ❌ **MLM** (Tiens, Vestige, GoldQuest, anything with "downline") — math is rigged against you
- ❌ **Get-rich-quick courses** for 10–50k BDT — recycled content at 100x markup
- ❌ **Anyone showing >15% guaranteed returns** — lying or scamming

### Skip for now (revisit at Stage 4+ if interested)
- ⏸ Options trading, futures, derivatives — too advanced + risky for current stage
- ⏸ Property flipping — needs capital you don't have + risk you can't take
- ⏸ Active stock picking with leverage / margin — actively dangerous

### Skip until specifically needed
- 🔒 Tax planning beyond basics — wait until you have real income to optimize
- 🔒 Trust / estate structures — too far ahead
- 🔒 Corporate finance / M&A — interesting but not relevant to your life right now

## The learning trap to avoid

There's a common trap: people watch hundreds of hours of finance YouTube and feel like they're learning. They're not — they're entertaining themselves with finance topics. Real learning has these markers:

- You can explain the concept to someone else
- You've written your own version of it in your own words
- You've applied it (even in small ways) in your life
- You can connect it to other concepts you've already learned

If hours of YouTube go by and you can't pass these tests, you're not learning — you're consuming. Switch to active learning: read → write → teach → apply.

---

## How to know you're learning fast enough

Honest progress markers:

| Week 4 | You can explain 4–6 foundational concepts |
| Week 8 | All 10 foundational concepts solid |
| Week 12 | You have 30+ days of real tracking data + can read your own numbers |
| Week 26 (6 mo) | You've raised your freelance rate at least once |
| Week 52 (1 yr) | You have a small (~10k+ BDT) emergency fund + understand investing in theory |
| Year 2 | You're investing small amounts in DPS/Sanchaypatra/stocks |
| Year 3 | Most debt cleared + real wealth knowledge + meaningful net worth growth |

If you're significantly behind these markers, ask yourself: am I learning, or just consuming?

If you're ahead — great, but check that you're not skipping stages (knowledge without practice is fragile).

## One sentence to remember

> **"You learn what you teach. You teach what you've practiced. You practice what you've decided is worth your time. Choose wisely."**
