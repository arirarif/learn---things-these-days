# 08 — Skill and Income Growth: From 90k to 3 lakh BDT / month

This is the Stage 2 deep-dive. Your freelance skill is your single biggest financial asset — bigger than any investment you'll ever make. Doubling your income over 24 months is realistic, doesn't require a single big lucky break, and makes everything downstream (savings, investing, eventual debt clearance) easier as a side-effect.

This file is what to actually *do* with the income side.

---

## The reality check first

### Why pure hourly freelancing has a ceiling
- Every dev has 8 productive hours/day, ~22 days/month = ~176 hours/month
- At even $20/hr (decent international rate), max is $3,520/month ≈ 3.85 lakh BDT
- But you don't bill 100% of hours — calls, scope changes, revisions, dead time eat 30–40%
- Realistic ceiling for hourly work: ~$15/hr × 120 billable hours = $1,800/month ≈ 2 lakh BDT

Hourly is fine to get *to* 2 lakh. To get past it, you need **leverage** — meaning each hour earns more than once.

### The three forms of leverage (Naval Ravikant's framework, applied to you)
1. **Capital** — money to make more money (you don't have this yet, so skip)
2. **Labor** — other people working for you (premature; come back at Stage 5)
3. **Code + Media** — software and content work while you sleep (this is your path)

You're already a developer. You can build software. That's your leverage.

## The 4-stage income ladder

### Rung 1 — Generalist hourly (where you probably are now)
- Upwork / Fiverr / direct local clients
- "I do WordPress / React / mobile app"
- Compete on price, race to the bottom
- Typical rate: $5–15/hr

### Rung 2 — Niche specialist
- Same hours, 2–3× rate, easier client acquisition
- "I do Shopify speed optimization for fashion DTC brands"
- Clients seek YOU instead of you bidding
- Typical rate: $25–60/hr

### Rung 3 — Productized service
- Same offering, fixed price, fixed timeline, repeatable delivery
- "$500 Shopify speed audit, 7 days, money-back if site doesn't pass Core Web Vitals"
- Stops being hourly — starts being a *product* with margin
- Scales because delivery becomes systematic

### Rung 4 — SaaS / digital product
- Software you build once, sell N times
- Monthly recurring revenue (MRR) compounds
- Real long-term wealth lives here

You should climb the ladder over 18–24 months. **Don't try to jump straight to Rung 4** — the rungs feed each other (niche work tells you what to productize, productized service reveals what SaaS to build).

## How to pick a niche (the highest-leverage decision)

Bad niches are too broad ("web dev for startups"). Good niches have three properties:

1. **Specific audience** with a specific budget
2. **Specific outcome** they're willing to pay for
3. **You can deliver** with your current skill stack

### Niche scoring formula
For any candidate niche, score it 1–5 on each:
- **Pain intensity** — how badly does this audience need this fixed?
- **Budget** — can they pay $1,000+ for this?
- **Reachability** — can you actually find these people?
- **Competition** — how many specialists already serve them?
- **Your edge** — do you bring something rare?

Total > 18 = pursue. Below 14 = skip.

### Niches that score well for a Bangladeshi web/app dev

| Niche | Why it works | Typical project value |
|---|---|---|
| Halal cosmetics / Muslim fashion DTC Shopify stores | Growing market, owners often less tech-savvy, cultural understanding is rare in Western devs | $1k–5k per project |
| Madrasa / Islamic school management software | Underserved, owners know they need it, recurring | $2k–10k + maintenance |
| Bangladesh real-estate listing / agent CRM | Local domain knowledge, dev capability is rare | $1k–5k + monthly retainer |
| WordPress speed + Core Web Vitals optimization | High-pain specialty, results measurable, can productize | $500–2k per site |
| Shopify → headless migration | Specialist work, premium pricing globally | $5k–15k per project |
| Stripe / Wise / payment integration for non-US founders | Global pain, you can charge globally | $1k–3k per integration |
| AI integration for SMB websites (chatbots, summaries) | New market, owners hear the noise but don't know how | $500–3k per project |
| Mobile apps for Islamic content / utilities | Recurring user base, can monetize ads + subscription | Variable |

Pick **1** niche. Not 3, not "I'll try a few." One. Two niches = no niche.

### How to test a niche cheaply
1. Write 10 LinkedIn / Twitter posts about problems in that niche, observe engagement
2. Cold-email 20 prospects offering a free audit / sample fix
3. If 3+ respond and 1 pays — niche is real
4. If 0 respond — niche is wrong or pitch is wrong; iterate

Budget for this test: **1 month, $0 spent**. Just your time.

## How to raise rates without losing clients

This is the most common mistake — devs feel guilty raising rates. They shouldn't.

### Rate-raise mechanics
- **New clients** always get the new rate. Always. No exception.
- **Existing clients** — raise rates 20% every 12 months, with 30 days written notice. Most stay. The ones who leave were not your good clients anyway.
- **Quote in $ not in BDT** — gets you off the local floor
- **Quote ranges** ("$2,000–$3,500 depending on scope") instead of single numbers — anchors higher
- **Tie price to outcome** ("$3k for a checkout that converts +20%") not effort ("$3k for 60 hours of work")

### Rate progression — realistic 18-month plan
| Month | New-client rate | Existing-client rate |
|---|---|---|
| 0 (now) | $10/hr | $5–10/hr |
| 3 | $15/hr | $10/hr |
| 6 | $25/hr | $15/hr |
| 9 | $40/hr or fixed-price | $20/hr |
| 12 | $50/hr or productized | $25/hr |
| 18 | $75/hr or productized | $40/hr or replaced |

If you double the rate AND keep half your hours, you're already at the same income with half the time freed up for SaaS/productization work.

## Productizing (Rung 3)

A productized service is a service that *looks like a product*: fixed scope, fixed price, fixed delivery time. The buyer knows exactly what they get for exactly what they pay.

### Template
- **Name:** [Audience]'s [Pain]-Fix
- **What you get:** specific deliverable list
- **Price:** fixed
- **Time:** fixed
- **Guarantee:** specific outcome or refund

### Example for your skill stack
> **"Halal Brand Shopify Launchpad"**
> A complete Shopify store setup for halal fashion / cosmetics brands.
> What you get: brand-aligned theme, 20 product uploads, Stripe + bKash payments, halal-certification badges, page-speed under 2s.
> Time: 14 days from kickoff
> Price: $1,500 (or 1.5 lakh BDT)
> Guarantee: launch in 14 days or 100% refund

Sell 2/month = ~3 lakh BDT/month with predictable workload.

### Where to sell productized services
- LinkedIn (post weekly about the niche problems you solve)
- Twitter / X (build in public — show audits, before/afters)
- Niche subreddits (r/shopify, r/wordpress, r/startups — give value, mention the product in your bio)
- Cold email (personalized, with a 30-second audit video attached)
- Productized Service directory: **producti.fyi**, **productizeyourself.com**

## Building a SaaS (Rung 4) — small bets approach

You're not Stripe or Figma. You're a solo developer. The model that works is **micro-SaaS**: a tiny, sharp tool that 100–1,000 paying users find worth $5–30/month.

### Rules for your first SaaS
1. Build for a niche you've already served as a freelancer (you know the pain)
2. Solve **one** problem, not five
3. Ship in **4–8 weeks**, not 12 months
4. Charge from **day one** — free users teach you nothing
5. Aim for **$1,000 MRR** as the first milestone (50 users × $20/mo)
6. Build in public — tweet progress weekly

### Concrete micro-SaaS ideas for your profile

| Idea | Audience | Pricing | Why it works |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Halal Stock Screener for DSE** | Bangladeshi Muslim retail investors | 200 BDT/month | Real pain, no competitor, you already need it for yourself |
| **Zakat Calculator + Annual Tracker** | Muslim professionals worldwide | $3/month or $30/year | Massive audience, simple build |
| **Madrasa Fee Management** | 1000s of madrasas in Bangladesh | 500 BDT/month | Underserved, sticky once installed |
| **Bengali Invoice Generator for Freelancers** | Bangladeshi freelancers (you know this pain) | 100 BDT/month | You are the customer |
| **Halal Travel / Restaurant Finder API** | App developers building Muslim-focused apps | $20/month API access | B2B = higher willingness to pay |
| **WordPress Page Speed Audit Tool** | Agencies, web devs | $15/month | Productize your audit service |
| **Shopify Halal Certification Badge App** | Halal e-commerce stores worldwide | $10/month | Listed on Shopify App Store = built-in distribution |

Pick one. Build a v1 in 6 weeks. Charge from day one. If after 3 months you have 0 paying users, kill it and try the next. Don't fall in love with the idea — fall in love with the iteration.

### Where to launch a small SaaS
- **Product Hunt** — for English-speaking audience
- **Indie Hackers** community
- **r/SaaS, r/Entrepreneur** on Reddit
- **Twitter build-in-public** community (huge for solo devs)
- **Hacker News "Show HN"**
- **Bangladeshi Facebook groups** for local-niche products
- **LinkedIn** for B2B SaaS

## The non-negotiable habits for income growth

1. **Every Sunday — 30 minutes:** raise something. Write a niche post. Update a portfolio piece. Email 5 prospects. Anything that improves perceived value.
2. **Every quarter — quote new clients 20% higher than last quarter.** Force discipline.
3. **Every Friday — 1 hour:** invest in skill upgrade (1 new technique, 1 new framework, 1 new sales tactic)
4. **Never work below your floor rate again** once you've raised it. Floor goes up only.
